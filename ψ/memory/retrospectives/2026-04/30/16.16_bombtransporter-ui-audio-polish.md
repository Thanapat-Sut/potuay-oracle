## AI Diary

This was a marathon session where I watched a game go from skeleton to polished. The BombTransporter needed everything — UI, audio, animation, input fixes. What struck me most was the bug at the end: FlashTeamSide stacking explosion colors. It was a classic race condition where Flash() captures _sr.color as "original" but the original was already corrupted by a previous incomplete flash. The Pilot caught it visually — "the floor is red after 30 seconds" — and the screenshot told me immediately what happened. 11 hits on one team means 11 overlapping Flash coroutines, each one capturing a progressively more red "original" color.

The fix was straightforward once diagnosed: skip non-Floor tiles (let their own routines handle visuals) and force-reset Floor tiles to their true base color before starting a new Flash. This is the same pattern we learned from IceLand — block at the source, don't compensate downstream. The particle system was fun to build — pure UI-based, no ParticleSystem component, just runtime GameObjects with Image and coroutine-driven animation. HDR colors (2-3x multiplier) make them bloom nicely with post-processing.

The audio flow had a nice subtlety: BGM plays from Setup through Waiting, fades out during countdown (where CountdownSFX takes over), then fades back in for Playing. This gives the countdown moment a clean audio break that makes it feel intentional.

## Lessons Learned

- Flash coroutine stacking: when Flash() captures `orig = _sr.color`, rapid re-invocation creates color drift. Always reset to known base color before starting a new flash.
- UI particle systems work well without ParticleSystem component — runtime Image + coroutine is simpler for 2D UI effects.
- BGM fade-out during countdown creates a clean audio break that makes the countdown feel more impactful.
